Fortinet Unified SASE — Complete Product Portfolio, Roles, Architecture, Licensing & Industry Use Cases

1What is Fortinet SASE?

SASE (Secure Access Service Edge) collapses the traditional "data-center-in-the-middle" hub-and-spoke model into a globally distributed cloud fabric. Users connect to the nearest Point of Presence (PoP), where their traffic is inspected by a full security stack, then forwarded to the internet, SaaS, or private applications. Fortinet delivers this as Unified SASE — converging its market-leading Secure SD-WAN with a cloud-delivered Security Service Edge (SSE).

🌐 Single Operating System

Every form factor — physical FortiGate, virtual FortiGate, and the FortiSASE cloud PoP — runs FortiOS. Policies, objects and inspection engines are identical edge-to-cloud (the "Hybrid Mesh Firewall").

👤 Single Agent

FortiClient (the FortiSASE agent) delivers endpoint protection, VPN, ZTNA posture, SWG and Digital Experience Monitoring from one lightweight install on managed devices.

🧠 Single Intelligence

FortiGuard Labs feeds real-time threat intel (AV, IPS, web/DNS filtering, anti-botnet, inline sandboxing, AI/ML) to every PoP and edge device under subscription.

The three pillars of SSE inside FortiSASE: Secure Internet Access (SIA) replaces the on-prem proxy/firewall for web & internet traffic; Secure Private Access (SPA) replaces VPN with ZTNA + SD-WAN to reach private apps; Secure SaaS Access uses inline + API CASB to govern sanctioned and shadow SaaS.

5Service Connectivity — SIA · SPA · Secure SaaS

FortiSASE delivers three connectivity "services," each solving a different access problem. Most deployments enable all three under one per-user license.

Figure 3 — The three service paths share one PoP inspection stack and one license.

🌐 SIA — Secure Internet Access

Problem solved: backhauling internet traffic to a central firewall is slow & costly.

  • SWG, FWaaS, DNS security, IPS, AV, DLP, inline sandbox
  • Direct, inspected internet breakout from the nearest PoP
  • Replaces on-prem proxy / VPN-hairpinning

☁ Secure SaaS Access

Problem solved: no visibility/control over sanctioned & shadow SaaS.

  • Inline CASB for real-time policy & DLP
  • API CASB scans data-at-rest in tenants
  • Shadow-IT discovery & risk scoring
  • Tenant restrictions (block personal logins)

🏢 SPA — Secure Private Access

Problem solved: VPN gives flat, over-broad network access.

  • ZTNA: per-app, posture-checked, no lateral movement
  • SD-WAN overlay to FortiGate hub for legacy apps
  • Reaches DC, private cloud & IaaS workloads

7Sovereign SASE — Data Sovereignty & Deployment Control

Sovereign SASE gives an organization full control over where the SASE security stack runs and where its traffic, logs and inspection happen — keeping data inside a chosen jurisdiction or entirely under the organization's own control, instead of inside vendor-operated, vendor-located shared cloud PoPs. It is the answer to data-residency laws and the needs of governments, defense, and critical-infrastructure operators.

The core idea: standard cloud SASE routes your traffic, logs and metadata through the vendor's multi-tenant PoPs in vendor-chosen countries. Sovereign SASE lets you move that enforcement and data into your jurisdiction — or fully onto your own infrastructure — without changing the security stack, because every form factor runs the same FortiOS.

The deployment spectrum — same stack, three sovereignty levels

Figure 5 — Sovereign SASE spectrum: the same FortiOS stack, hosted Fortinet-side, by a local provider, or fully self-hosted.
Convenience-first (Fortinet-hosted) In-country managed (SP-hosted) Full control (self-hosted)

What you control with Sovereign SASE

🗺️ Data residency

Traffic, logs and metadata stay in your chosen country/region — meeting localization mandates and avoiding cross-border data transfer.

🏛️ Infrastructure ownership

Choose who owns and operates the PoP — Fortinet, a vetted local provider, or your own team on your own hardware/cloud.

🔑 Keys & inspection points

Retain control of encryption keys and where SSL/TLS decryption and inspection occur — no third party sees cleartext.

📜 Regulatory compliance

Satisfy GDPR, national data laws, defense and critical-infrastructure mandates with auditable, in-jurisdiction enforcement.

🧠 Threat-intel control

Use FortiGuard, local feeds, or a hybrid — and govern exactly what telemetry (if any) leaves your environment.

🔒 Air-gap capable

Self-hosted mode supports isolated/offline environments for the most sensitive networks (defense, OT, intelligence).

Commercial note: Sovereign SASE generally keeps the same per-user subscription model, plus the relevant deployment component — self-hosted infrastructure costs (compute/licensing for the org-run stack) for the full-sovereign model, or a local provider's managed fee for the service-provider-hosted model. 9Licensing Model

FortiSASE is licensed as a per-user, annual subscription sold in user-count tiers. The base subscription bundles the SSE security stack; capacity, sites and dedicated resources are add-ons.

Figure 6 — Licensing = base per-user subscription × edition tier + capacity/site add-ons.

User-Count Tiers (illustrative)

TierUser bandTypical segmentWhat's included
Entry25–250 usersSMB / single departmentBase SSE bundle, shared PoP capacity
Mid250–2,000 usersMid-market / multi-siteAdds SD-WAN connectivity, CASB/DLP, DEM
Enterprise2,000–10,000+ usersLarge enterpriseDedicated IPs, multi-region, premium support, FortiManager/Analyzer integration
Licensing principles to remember:
① The user is the unit of value — a user is covered on any device, anywhere.
② Security services are bundled into the subscription edition, not bought à-la-carte like on a hardware FortiGate.
③ Sites (thin-edge) and bandwidth/throughput for private access are the main capacity add-ons.
④ FortiGate hubs in your DC/cloud still need their own FortiGate license + FortiGuard bundle (UTP or Enterprise Protection).
⑤ Terms are typically 1 / 3 / 5-year prepaid; longer terms lower the effective per-user/month rate.

13Summary & Glossary

The one-paragraph summary: Fortinet Unified SASE converges Secure SD-WAN and a cloud-delivered SSE (FWaaS, SWG, ZTNA, CASB, DLP, IPS, sandbox, DEM) on a single OS (FortiOS), single agent (FortiClient), single management plane and single threat-intel source (FortiGuard). Users and sites connect to the nearest PoP via SIA, SPA and Secure SaaS Access; private apps are reached through a FortiGate SD-WAN hub or ZTNA proxy. It's licensed per user, per year, with site & bandwidth add-ons — shifting security spend from per-site CapEx to predictable OpEx while consolidating multiple point-products into one vendor.
